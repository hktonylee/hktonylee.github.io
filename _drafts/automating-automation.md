## Automation never felt small

- Automation never felt like a small productivity trick to me.
- The meaning of automation kept expanding throughout my career.
- Each phase changed what I thought engineers were really building.

## When automation meant removing repetition

- Early on, automation meant escaping repetitive manual engineering work.
- Small scripts, shortcuts, and utilities created disproportionate leverage.
- The first lesson was simple: computers should absorb repetition faster than humans.

## When automation became systems

- Later, the target was no longer a single task but an entire workflow.
- Environments, integration points, deployments, and repeatability became the real surface area.
- Automation started to shape how teams worked, not just how I worked.

## When automation started helping build itself

- AI feels important because it shortens the distance between intent and implementation.
- It can help generate code, transformations, and scaffolding that used to require more manual construction.
- That makes it feel like automation is beginning to assist in creating more automation.

## What still belongs to engineers

- Engineers still define goals, constraints, and standards.
- Verification, judgment, and taste still matter.
- Better automation increases responsibility rather than removing it.

## Closing

- The direction feels optimistic to me.
- Engineers are moving upward in abstraction and responsibility.
- The long arc of automation seems to point toward automating its own creation.
