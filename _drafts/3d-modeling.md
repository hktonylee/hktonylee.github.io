Recently I have bought a 3D printer.

I still remembered when I first read the book [Makers: The New Industrial Revolution](https://www.amazon.ca/Makers-Industrial-Revolution-Chris-Anderson/dp/0307720969) 10+ years ago. I was fascinated by the content - it first foresee the rise of the makers. People will DIY things in their home, due to the improvement of the technologies and how people share their knowledge. People just click and create their stuff in their home, or a 3D print store near their home.

It seem the time has come.

Now it costs around 50 USD to print a shoe that perfectly suits your feet. And the cost is dropping. If more talent and capital starts to look into this, I would say this can totally change the shoe business. Imagine when you walk into a shoe shop, you do not need to pick a shoe and try. You just pick the style, and measure your feet. The magic mirror (some OLED + AR + Generative AI, but in mirror) will project the shoe on your feet. If you think it looks good, you can just place the order and come back later to pick up. The shoe will 100% match your feet.

It will also blur, or shrink, the boundary of product. Product is a concept from the industrial world. It means something (physical, or digital) that is reliably and perfectly produced. The main reason of doing that is to cut cost down. But with those 3D printers and generative AI, now we have comparatively low-cost customization. If the cost keeps cutting down, to some point, people will demand more personalized version rather than mass produced version. The boundry product will be shrinked to things that absolutely require mass production.

Let's go back to the 3D printer that I bought.

It's a Bambu Lab P1S printer. It is proven to be very reliable, low cost and useful. And I found it to be nice looking.

The first thing that I printed (and I would recommend every new owner of the 3D printer) is to print a poop basket.
