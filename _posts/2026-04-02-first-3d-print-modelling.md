3D printing has been getting more popular recently. I saw an advertisement from some manufacturer in China offering super low-cost 3D printing, something like $2 for a small item including shipping. Plus, I had some free time. Why not give it a try?

I started learning Autodesk Fusion. The last time I used CAD software was about 20 years ago, back in high school. I did not expect to use CAD software again after that, but here I am ;)

To begin with, I modeled something small: a drain filter. I had seen someone create a drain that never gets blocked, and I thought this would be a good way to start with a simple project like this.

I re-learned the basics of CAD, like creating constrained sketches, using splines, and making solids. I also had to deal with some design issues, such as walls that were too thin. After some back and forth, the manufacturer finally accepted the model and started printing it.

After two weeks, I received the print in a nice box, and the print quality was better than I expected!
