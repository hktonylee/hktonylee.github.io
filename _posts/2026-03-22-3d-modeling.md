Recently I bought a 3D printer.

I still remember when I first read the book [Makers: The New Industrial Revolution](https://www.amazon.ca/Makers-Industrial-Revolution-Chris-Anderson/dp/0307720969) 10+ years ago. I was fascinated by the content—it foresaw the rise of makers. People would DIY things at home, thanks to improving technologies and how people share knowledge. People could just click and create their stuff at home, or at a 3D print store near their home.

It seems the time has come.

Now it costs around 50 USD to print a shoe that perfectly suits your feet. And the cost is dropping. If more talent and capital start to look into this, I would say this can totally change the shoe business. Imagine when you walk into a shoe shop, you do not need to pick a shoe and try it on. You just pick the style, and measure your feet. The magic mirror (some OLED + AR + Generative AI, but in a mirror) will project the shoe on your feet. If you think it looks good, you can just place the order and come back later to pick it up. The shoe will 100% match your feet.

It will also blur, or shrink, the boundary of product. Product is a concept from the industrial world. It means something (physical, or digital) that is reliably and perfectly produced. The main reason for doing that is to cut cost. But with those 3D printers and generative AI, now we have comparatively low-cost customization. If the cost keeps dropping, at some point, people will demand more personalized versions rather than mass-produced versions. The boundary of product will be shrunk to things that absolutely require mass production.

Let's go back to the 3D printer that I bought.

It's a Bambu Lab P1S printer. It is proven to be very reliable, low cost and useful. And I find it nice looking.

The first thing that I printed (and I would recommend every new owner of a 3D printer) is a poop basket.

![IMG_6615](/Users/tonylee/Downloads/Safari/IMG_6615.jpeg)

It printed so well and so easily. I just downloaded the model from the Internet and printed it, with only several clicks.

After that, I started to learn more about 3D printing, like the slicer settings, materials handling, and 3D modeling. I will post some of the projects that I am working on later.
