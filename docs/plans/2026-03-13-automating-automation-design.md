# Automating Automation Design

## Summary

This post is aimed at other engineers. It will use a career-to-thesis narrative to show how my understanding of automation evolved over time, culminating in the idea that the ultimate goal of automation is to automate itself.

The article should feel reflective, concrete, and optimistic. It should avoid generic AI hype and instead present AI as a continuation of a long engineering trend: shortening the distance between intent and implementation.

## Audience

Engineers who care about software, systems, tooling, and the long-term direction of automation.

## Core Thesis

Automation does not stop at automating repetitive tasks. As automation matures, it increasingly helps create more automation. AI is important because it materially assists with the construction of code, workflows, transformations, and systems that previously required more manual engineering effort.

In short: the direction of automation is toward automating its own creation.

## Tone

- Personal but not memoir-like
- Technical enough to feel credible to engineers
- Optimistic without sounding naive
- Reflective rather than predictive

## Recommended Structure

1. Opening hook
   Explain that automation never felt like just a way to save time. Frame it as an expanding idea that kept changing throughout my career.
2. Early career phase
   Describe a stage where automation meant removing repetitive manual effort from day-to-day engineering work.
3. Mid-career phase
   Show how the focus widened from tasks to systems, workflows, environments, and repeatable processes.
4. AI phase
   Explain why AI feels like the next logical stage: it can help generate, connect, and refine the automation itself.
5. Thesis with caution
   Argue that automating automation is the direction, while clarifying that human judgment, constraints, and taste still matter.
6. Closing reflection
   End on an optimistic point: engineers are not disappearing, but moving upward toward defining intent, choosing constraints, and shaping what should be automated next.

## Argument Framing

The article should distinguish between two phases:

- Traditional automation: build a machine or script to do the work
- Emerging automation: build a system that helps build the next machine or script

This framing makes the AI section less hype-driven and more historically grounded.

## Writing Constraints

- Keep the career section relevant to the thesis
- Do not turn the piece into a general overview of AI trends
- Use concrete examples from my own experience where possible
- Prefer clear progression over broad coverage
- End with conviction, not anxiety
